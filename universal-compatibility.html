Mechanism 6:
Universal Compatibility.

Most nanocarbon dispersions are optimized for one polymer system — and perform unpredictably in others. Flexiphene™ is a platform technology: the surfactant-free, high-functional-content Flexiphene™ integrates into thermoplastics, thermosets, elastomers, and coatings without requiring changes to your existing process chemistry. If you can add a liquid to your process, you can add Flexiphene™.

Compatible Systems

Validated Across Polymer Families

Flexiphene™'s Flexiphene™ works across all major polymer classes. The functional groups on the nanocarbon component interact favorably with both polar and nonpolar matrices; the surfactant-free surface avoids the compatibility issues caused by surfactant selection mismatch in specific polymer systems.

🏭

Engineering Thermoplastics

PA 66, PEEK, PPS, PEI, and other high-performance thermoplastics used in automotive, aerospace, and industrial applications. Published ASTM data available for PA 66 at 1 wt.%.

🔧

Commodity Thermoplastics

PP, PE, PET, PVC, and commodity engineering plastics where nanocarbon additives improve mechanical performance, flame retardancy, or electrical conductivity for value-added applications.

🧱

Thermoset Resins

Epoxy, vinyl ester, phenolic, cyanate ester, and BMI resin systems for composite manufacturing. Compatible with wet layup, VARTM, RTM, and prepreg processing methods.

🤸

Elastomers & Rubbers

Polyurethane, silicone, EPDM, and natural rubber systems for flexible, damping, and sealing applications. Nanocarbon improves mechanical strength without compromising elastomeric behavior.

🎨

Coatings & Inks

Water-based and solvent-based coating formulations, conductive inks, and functional coatings. The liquid dispersion format integrates directly into coating formulation workflows.

📐

Composite Matrices

CFRP, GFRP, and hybrid fiber-reinforced composite matrices for aerospace, defense, and high-performance structural applications. Reinforces the matrix and fiber-matrix interface simultaneously.

Integration Methods

Works With Your Existing Process

Flexiphene™ ships as a ready-to-use liquid dispersion. No powder handling. No special equipment. No process overhaul required. It integrates into the processing step that makes the most sense for your system.

①

Melt Compounding

For thermoplastics: add Flexiphene™ dispersion as a liquid feed during twin-screw extrusion, or pre-mix with polymer pellets before compounding. The liquid evaporates during processing; nanocarbon integrates into the polymer melt.

②

Solution Mixing

For solution-cast films and coatings: add Flexiphene™ to the polymer solution before casting. Homogeneous mixing is straightforward due to the stable liquid dispersion format.

③

Resin Infusion

For thermoset composites: add Flexiphene™ to the base resin before mixing with hardener. Compatible with VARTM, RTM, and manual infusion processes. Viscosity impact is minimal at 1 wt.%.

④

Wet Layup

For hand layup composites: pre-mix with the resin component. Nanocarbon disperses throughout the matrix during layup without requiring modifications to layup tooling or procedures.

⑤

Coating Application

For films and coatings: add Flexiphene™ to the coating formulation before application. Compatible with spray, spin, dip, and draw-down coating methods. Film properties controlled by dispersion concentration and deposition parameters.

⑥

Buckypaper Filtration

For standalone nanocarbon films: filter through membrane to form buckypaper. The surfactant-free dispersion produces papers with higher conductivity and cleaner surfaces than surfactant-based alternatives without a washing step.
